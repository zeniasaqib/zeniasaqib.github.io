About

I am currently a senior at Mount Holyoke College, studying Computer Science and Political Science. I intend to graduate in May 2020 with a Bachelor of Arts degree.

I worked as a Political Intern for the Office of U.S. Senator Elizabeth Warren in Summer 2017, and as a Virtual Learning and Development Intern at New York Life in Summer 2019. Through these experiences, I have developed a passion for impact driven work and development.

Technology should be a means to achieve equity and accessibility in society. I believe that by democratizing tech and integrating diversity, inclusion, and focused educational initiatives into the very fabric of the tech industry, we can ensure that future products and services work for EVERYONE.

I am currently working on building a recommender system in Python as an exploration of algorithmic bias in tech.

Inspired by the work of

  • Safiya Noble: Algorithms of Oppression: How Search Engines Reinforce Racism
  • Cathy O'Neil: Weapons of Math Destruction: How Big Data Increases Inequality and Threatens Democracy (I got to meet the author at a talk at Mount Holyoke!)
  • Kris Shaffer: Data versus Democracy: How Big Data Algorithms Shape Opinions and Alter the Course of History
  • Tobias Baer: Understand, Manage, and Prevent Algorithmic Bias: A Guide for Business Users and Data Scientists

    • Experience

    HIGH SCHOOL:

    I took the initiative to start a music mentorship program for beginner band & orchestra students in the Westborough School District. The program was a success. 40 elementary school and high school students were paired up in the first year of the program, and every single one of the beginner players went on the proceed with the program the following year! I also made a set of videos for the Westborough Fine Arts Department to show as part of their program recruitment efforts. I won a Girl Scout Gold Award for this project in 2016, and presented my work at the Massachusetts State House.

    This experience taught me that making information and resources accessible truly makes a difference in community development. I hope to take my experience from this project and apply it to future educational initiatives in tech.

    SUMMER 2017:

    I interned at the Office of U.S. Senator Elizabeth Warren during the summer before sophomore year. During this experience, I worked with the Regional Director of South Shore to research policy development in the area. I was also in charge of constituent services, and was responsible for engaging with local politicians and situations, in addition to forwarding congressional inquiries. I was most people's first point of contact with the office.

    I also assisted with the Senator's campaign efforts, and have attended several Town Hall events as a team member. These experiences taught me the importance of a network, and the logistics of getting work done at a state and federal level.
